I am a second-year PhD student at the University of Texas at Austin studying linguistics with a focus on sign languages. My particular interests are currently on modality and contact between different languages. I am also very interested in learning about psycholinguistics, sociolinguistics, and specific elements of sign language historical linguistics such as manual codes of spoken languages, initialization, and contact between different sign languages. 

I was born and raised about an hour south of Boston, Massachusetts, and attended undergrad at Boston University, double-majoring in Psychology and Linguistics, and eventually getting a simultaneous MA in Linguistics. Many people ask me what I think about Austin, Texas, having lived in Massachusetts my whole life. It is very hot here. My native language is English, although, at this point, I can make my way through conversations in Spanish and in American Sign Language (ASL). I would like to get much better at both of those languages. In my free time, I enjoy creative writing, crafts, Magic: the Gathering, explaining Sonic the Hedgehog storylines to others, and baking. 

I typically avoid most social media, especially in a professional capacity. If you would like to contact me, I would encourage you to email me through my contact info on this website.
